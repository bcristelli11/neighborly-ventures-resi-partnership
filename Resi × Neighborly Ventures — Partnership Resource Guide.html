Questions and answers

Pulled directly from our discovery conversation — answers to what Rachael's team asked and what leadership will likely ask next.

Migration and onboarding

How much internal lift does the G5 migration require?

The burden of migration is on Resi. We pre-fill your onboarding by scraping your existing G5 sites — pulling copy, structure, and content so your team isn't starting from scratch for 20 properties. You review and approve; you don't rebuild. Asset collection (photos, logos, brand elements) typically takes 1–2 days per phase. From there, Resi handles the build, integration, and QA. For a three-person team with a July decision window and September go-live target, this is how we make the timeline achievable.

Does Resi integrate cleanly with Resman?

Resman is a native integration. Pricing, availability, and unit data sync automatically with configurable overrides for marketing-specific adjustments. All sync logs are visible in the platform, and you control sync frequency directly — no manual data pulls, no spreadsheet reconciliation, no support ticket required to check status.

How does Elise AI connect into the platform?

Elise AI connects as a CRM integration through the integrations hub. Lead source data captured through Resi forms (contact, tour scheduling, general inquiry) carries through to Elise AI with UTM and source attribution intact — so CRM records reflect where the lead actually came from, not just "website." You manage integration settings directly in the platform without involving Resi support for routine adjustments.

What's the turnaround for a new property after the initial rollout?

Launch theme: 3–5 business days post-asset collection. Premium: 7–10 days. Resi pre-fills onboarding using public information from the existing property listing or site, which compresses the collection phase significantly. New properties for existing partners are treated as priority builds — no queue delay.

SEO and content

Do you manage Google Business Profile, and do you handle GBP posting?

GBP management is included in SEO services. At the Launch tier, Resi handles a one-time GBP optimization — keyword-enriched descriptions, Q&A setup, floor plans as products (where available), and social channel linking. At the Premium tier, that expands to include live pricing connected directly to GBP and monthly new SEO content pages that build compounding local visibility over time.

GBP posting isn't included in Launch or Premium — it's available at the Pro tier. That said, Resi is actively exploring a partnership with Maven AI to bring automated GBP posting into the platform. Neighborly Ventures is already using Maven AI on one property and has expressed interest in piloting this together as the integration develops.

How do you approach GEO — generative engine optimization?

GEO strategy centers on content that meets Google's "helpful, authoritative, and trustworthy" standards. Multifamily now falls under Google's YMYL designation, which requires stronger E-E-A-T signals. We proactively build those into every SEO program with "Meet the Team" pages, robust neighborhood content, rent table disclosures, and resident review campaigns. Neighborhood pages update dynamically via Google data — local content stays current without manual updates, and positions properties well for AI-generated search responses.

How do you handle concessions and special announcements across the portfolio?

Announcements — banners, header bars — are managed in the CMS with scheduled start and stop dates. Set them once, apply across individual properties or push to the full portfolio simultaneously. ILS syndication to Apartments.com, Zillow, Zumper, and Apartment List means specials propagate from one source, one time.

Can you share an example of adjusting strategy when performance isn't hitting goals?

For a luxury lease-up in Las Vegas, organic content focused on Las Vegas proper was attracting high-volatility traffic. We shifted targeting to neighboring submarkets to reach more stable, qualified renters — and saw stronger conversion rates as a result. For a student housing client, we reallocated budget from top-of-funnel to lower-funnel campaigns and accelerated leases before move-in. The key is diagnosing where in the funnel the breakdown is occurring and making targeted adjustments from there, not waiting for a quarterly review to flag the problem.

Digital advertising

Do you offer call scoring — AI-scored intent-to-rent analysis on calls?

Resi doesn't offer call scoring. Instead, Resi provides lead attribution and DNI: call and email tracking that follows a lead source from the referral site all the way through to Resman and Elise AI — so CRM records show where leads actually came from, not just "website." Most Resi clients rely on native call recording within their CRM for call quality insights rather than a separate scoring layer. Neighborly Ventures currently pays for call scoring with G5 but noted it isn't actively used as a KPI — so this is unlikely to be a gap in practice.

Can we pause or reduce ad spend on stabilized properties?

Yes — and we'll recommend it when the data supports it. If a property is trending at 96%+ occupied, we'd suggest reallocating that budget to properties with more availability or stronger seasonal demand. Budget recommendations are driven by Resman occupancy data and six-month trends, not spend targets. Any adjustment at the property level doesn't affect pricing or services elsewhere in the portfolio.

Do you recommend sending PPC traffic to the website or separate landing pages?

Always to the website. Every ad dollar that drives traffic to your site builds domain authority. You can build targeted landing pages — 2BR-specific, lease-up, specials — directly in the platform using the drag-and-drop builder, on your domain. No external page, no authority going somewhere you don't own, no separate vendor to manage.

How are budget recommendations generated?

Our model takes current occupancy and the six-month trend, cross-referenced with market benchmarks. Availability pulls directly from Resman via the integration. We provide three tiers — conservative, optimal, and aggressive — for every property, and refresh recommendations monthly alongside performance reviews. Budget decisions stay with Neighborly Ventures; we give you the data to make them confidently.

Design and day-to-day operations

Is website copywriting included in the build cost, or is that a separate fee?

Keyword-rich, SEO-optimized copy is included in the build — homepage, amenities, and key pages. What's not included is branded creative copywriting: if Neighborly Ventures has a specific tone, voice, or messaging that needs to come through, that direction comes from your team. Resi integrates it. Everything is bundled into the one-time build cost with no separate line item for copy.

Do you provide stock images, or do we need to supply our own photos?

Resi can provide a handful of stock images per site for minor use — filling gaps where needed. Deep or curated photo galleries aren't something Resi sources. For brand-aligned imagery, client-supplied photography is the best path. If there are specific images you want used, share them and Resi will incorporate them. Unlike G5, there's no additional charge for stock image use.

G5 charges $2,000 for a "tailored aesthetic package" — branded icons, patterns, and graphic accents. Is that included with Resi?

Yes, and there's no extra charge for it. Branding-level design — logo application, typography, borders, backgrounds, and graphic accents — is included in both Launch and Premium themes. The elements G5 breaks out as a $2,000 upsell are part of Resi's standard build. Dyverse had the same reaction when Rachael asked them about it.

Are Resi websites WCAG and ADA compliant at launch? Do you offer ongoing compliance audits?

Every site Resi builds and launches meets WCAG 2AA and ADA standards from day one. All platform elements in the visual builder are built to those same standards. Ongoing compliance can't be guaranteed for changes made independently by your team — if an edit introduces an issue, that falls outside what Resi can control. Proactive audits aren't offered, but the baseline Resi sets at launch is solid, and compliance issues from routine content edits are rare in practice.

Can each property look different while managed from one platform?

That's exactly the point of Resi-Made Themes. Each of your 20 conventional properties can use a different theme with its own color palette, typography, and layout structure — all managed from one login. Global content (FAQs, announcements, media) pushes across properties simultaneously, while property-specific content is managed at the site level. Portfolio efficiency without the copy-paste website problem.

What does the day-to-day support and ticketing experience look like?

Tickets are submitted through a click-and-comment system built directly into the platform. Highlight the element you need changed, leave a comment, submit. The ticket routes to Resi's CX team — not an outsourced queue — and status is visible in real time. Resolution typically happens in 24–48 hours. That's the difference between a support model that removes friction and a vendor relationship that creates it.
